Major Research

Student’s Academic Performance Prediction through Machine Learning

Air University

In this research, we discover which factor affect a student’s performance and what machine learning techniques can predict their performance with higher accuracy, aiming to produce qualitative results for student’s assessment and counselling. Comparative assessment was performed by five popular classification technique that is Logistic regression, K-nearest- neighbour, Naïve Bayes, CART (Classification and Regression Trees) and Random forest. The dataset used in this experiment was obtained from students and their identities were kept anonymous. Feature selection techniques were used to select the variables that have highest impact and performance of each technique was evaluated.

Online Student Learning Assessment through Natural Language Understanding

IBM and Deakin University

The purpose of this research was to evaluate an alternative way of screening self-reflection using IBM Natural Language Understanding based specific classifier and several psychologies inspired analysis techniques to provide an overall assessment of the student’s skills and capabilities. It was to highlight specific situations of necessity and factors that, due to the lack of time in the evaluation process, can be underestimated or sometimes missed. Ultimately, we aimed to find whether an AI-assisted approach through Natural Language Processing that can help to design a better learning experience for the students. The research was done during my internship at Deakin University and is being planned to publish in IBM sponsored journal.

Deep Learning Recommendation System

Ford Motors and Deakin University

The main aim of this research was to follow through different research and investigate whether modern Deep Learning algorithms live up to its expectation. Different reviews have been given in support with experiments and this literature review provides an analysis of different practices, state of the industrial methodologies and current research. Citation Sentiment Analysis Tool

Air University

The main idea of the research was based on relationships between the scholarly articles that are based on bibliographic pointers. Extraction of these bibliographic pointers along with the reasons is a challenging task. A tool was developed automatically extract these reasons with reasonable accuracy, recall and F-measure. My research was awarded first at Airtech 2017 competition and the judges awarded the price upon several factors which included the project’s creativity and integrity. My research was later on published in Corporum which is a Journal of Corpus Linguistics.
